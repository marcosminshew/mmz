Basic R1 Release Mission & Goals Foundational release for future Polaris roadmap. Integrate offers/loyalty into ecommerce on a new ecommerce platform and extend a new visual look/feel across the banner + ecommerce assets. 
  • Take a big step toward unifying our digital experiences
  • Have coupons/offers available in ecommerce
  • Unify design language & navigation system between our digital experiences
  • Integrate user account & loyalty (gas, rewards, offers)
  • Launch a new ecommerce platform (if timing allows)
KPI's
  • Drive measurable in-store traffic through digital (digital attribution)
  • Ecommerce Revenue Growth
  • Improve technical metrics ie reduce page load performance
  • Increase redemptions for J4U
  • Customer retention (repeat purchasers)
  • Increase Delivery Customers
Key Business Decisions
  • Pricing > what do we show i.e. in-store vs. online?
  • CPG revenue > what are we able to do w/ “clips” etc.?
  • Umbrella loyalty program > how do we communicate this?
  • Resource gaps > will certain new features/capabilities require new resources?
  • Analytics > how do we track in-store traffic attributable to digital?
  • Drive Traffic > needs to be at forefront of our experience to get more customers into our stores

Assumptions

  • This is focused on digital properties only. The below requirements extend to web, mobile web, and native mobile digital experiences. 
  • The below requirements will be built on a new eCommerce platform (IBM websphere v8) + Adobe Experience Manager (CMS & DAM) integrated with Loyalty Platform (Nimbus & Copient).
  • This release will focus on 1 banner first, then be supported by rolling releases for all banners.
  • We will retain 2 mobile apps and 2 desktop experiences (banner & delivery).

Redesign:  major changes to interactions, flows, and structure.

Re-skin: apply design language to existing content and interactions.

Coupons

Browse/search, clip (add), save, and checkout integration.
  • The j4u couponing experience will only be available to signed in customers. There will be a state for non-signed in customers which explains the concept & value proposition of couponing all displayed using content blocks. 
  • The customer shall access a couponing experience which displays coupons they may be interested in based on the home store they have selected which is clearly displayed in the header navigation. 
  • A customer can search all available coupons by keyword + barcode scan (mobile only). 
  • A customer can browse coupons using major filters like: past purchase history, categories (special offers, fruits/veggies, bread & bakery, beverages), special events, most recent, and expiring. 
  • A first-time customer shall experience a "how it works" content block which displays prominently and describes how the couponing experience works for redemption in store or for delivery/pickup. The "how it works" feature will then become a less prominently but still accessible link for future reminding.
  • A customer can view a coupon information including the product name, product images, offer details, personalized pricing, and other text based disclaimers.
  • A customer can complete either "add to list" or "add to delivery order" for coupons. 
  • When a customer selects "add to list," provide a visual indication of where the list is stored in the UI. 
  • When a customer selects "add to list" as a first-time user, provide an overlay message that reminds them how the process works for redeeming/using coupons either in store-on line. 
  • A customer can view available coupons/offers at the product level (ie PDP page), so they can more easily associate/“clip” an offer to a delivery / DUG item in the shopping cart.
